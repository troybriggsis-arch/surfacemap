SurfaceMap — Quick Start

1. Add Videos — Drag & drop video files or click ⊕ Video. Supports MP4, WebM, MOV natively. AVI, MKV, WMV, FLV, and other formats are auto-converted to MP4 using built-in FFmpeg (first conversion downloads a ~25MB engine).

2. Add Surfaces — Click ⊕ Surface to create a quad. Each surface appears as a rectangle you can warp.

3. Assign Videos — In the sidebar, use the dropdown on each surface to assign a video source.

4. Warp & Position — Drag the corner handles (blue circles) to warp the video onto your projection surface. Hold Shift to constrain movement.

5. Masks — Select a mask type (Circle, Ellipse, Polygon, Freehand) on any surface, then click Edit to open the mask editor. Use feather to soften edges for blending.

6. Sync Playback — Set a Delay (in seconds) on each surface. Click ▶ Play All to reset and start all videos with their offsets. Videos loop automatically.

7. Output Window — Click ⧉ Output Window or press F to open a second window. Drag that window to your projector display and fullscreen it (Mac: click the green ⊕ button or Ctrl+⌘+F · Windows: F11). Your edit window stays fully interactive — all changes update the projector in real-time.

8. Bright Mode — Press B or click ☀ Bright to switch both the edit canvas and the output window to a bright background. This lets you see exactly where the projector is reaching on your physical surfaces — since black = no light projected.

Keyboard Shortcuts:

  • F — Toggle output window
  • B — Toggle bright canvas
  • S — Toggle surface snapping
  • L — Lock/unlock surfaces
  • Delete / Backspace — Remove selected surface
  • D — Duplicate selected surface
  • Space — Play/pause all videos
  • ⌘Z — Undo last corner/move change
  • ⌘S — Save project
  • ⌘O — Load project
  • ⌘+ / ⌘− — Zoom in / out
  • ⌘0 — Reset zoom to 100%

Pan & Zoom (trackpad):

  • Two-finger scroll — Pan around the canvas
  • Pinch — Zoom in / out (centered on cursor)
  • ⌥+click drag — Pan (with mouse)
  • Works in both the main canvas and the mask editor

Surface Management:

  • Double-click a surface name to rename it.
  • Use ↑↓ arrows on each surface to change z-order (render order).
  • Click ▭ to reset a warped surface back to a clean rectangle.
  • Click and drag inside a surface to move the whole quad.

Physical Setup Tips:

  • Connect your projector as a second display (extended, not mirrored).
  • Click ⧉ Output Window, drag it onto the projector display.
  • Mac: click the green ⊕ button or Ctrl+⌘+F to fullscreen. Windows: F11.
  • Use Bright mode to see the full projector throw while positioning surfaces.
  • All edits in the main window update the projector output in real-time.

Save & Load:

  • Save stores surface positions, warping, masks, delays, and opacity — not the video files themselves (they're too large).
  • To restore a project: import the same video files first, then load your .surfacemap file. Surfaces auto-reconnect to videos by filename.
  • Keep your video files and .surfacemap file together in one folder for easy reloading.
